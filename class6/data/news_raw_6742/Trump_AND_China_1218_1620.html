Donald Trump, Julian Assange: Your Wednesday Evening Briefing


BYLINE: KAREN ZRAICK and SANDRA STEVENSON


SECTION: BRIEFING


LENGTH: 793 words



HIGHLIGHT: Here's what you need to know at the end of the day.


(Want to get this briefing by email? Here's the sign-up.)

Good evening. Here's the latest.

1. President Obama went to Capitol Hill to urge Democrats to fight to maintain his signature health initiative, the Affordable Care Act.

But the bitter divide over the law will play out over the coming weeks, as Congressional Republicans turn to an arcane procedure called budget reconciliation to try to gut the law, which has allowed more than 20 million Americans to obtain health insurance. 

_____

2. Intelligence officials will brief Congress on their investigation of election-related hacking on Thursday, ahead of a briefing for President-elect Donald Trump on Friday.

Mr. Trump continued to raise questions about the intelligence pointing to Russian involvement, making common cause with Julian Assange, the founder of WikiLeaks.

And Ivanka Trump is apparently moving to Washington. People familiar with the decision say she and her husband, Jared Kushner, have picked a $5.5 million mansion previously owned by a Latvian-born financier who has extensive investments in Russia and ties to a Russian opposition leader.

_____

3. California lawmakers are girding for ideological battles with the Trump administration over immigration, the environment and criminal justice.

Lawmakers hired Eric Holder Jr., Mr. Obama's former attorney general, to represent them in the legal fights they expect with the new White House.

"We are very, very serious," the State Senate's Democratic leader said.

_____

4. Already need a break? Here's our roundup of 52 places to go in 2017.

The choices, culled from hundreds of recommendations from our globe-exploring travel writers, range from the bucolic and secluded (the Ryukyu Islands in Japan) to industrial urban landscapes (the South Bronx). No. 1 is close to home: Canada.

_____

5. Turkey extended emergency rule for three months, and officials said they had identified the fugitive gunman who carried out a deadly rampage at an Istanbul nightclub on New Year's Day.

President Recep Tayyip Erdogan insisted he would not let terrorism divide the nation. But Turkey's deep divisions are making unity difficult.

_____

6. Dylann Roof offered no apology or explanation for killing nine people at a historically black church in Charleston, S.C., in opening remarks in the sentencing phase of his trial.

During the massacre, Mr. Roof accused black people of raping "our women" and taking over the country. A prosecutor read from the jailhouse journal Mr. Roof kept in the weeks after: "I am not sorry. I have not shed a tear for the innocent people I killed."

Twelve jurors will decide whether he will be sentenced to death or life in prison.

_____

7. The city that supposedly never sleeps doesn't shoot as much as it used to. For the first time since at least the 1990s, New York City recorded fewer than 1,100 shootings in a calendar year. The 335 murders in 2016 almost tied the record low of 333.

That's a sharp contrast with Chicago - about half New York's size - which ended the year with 762 murders, the most in two decades, and more than 3,500 shootings.

_____

8. New "restorative justice" programs are allowing crime victims and their survivors to meet with the people who upended their lives. Offenders can try to come to terms with their acts - and victims or their families can finally ask, "Why?"

The couple above met with the drunken driver convicted of killing their 21-year-old son, with powerful results.

The mother's offer of a hug "changed my life," the man said.

_____

9. We visited Liberty City in Miami, to meet with the makers of "Moonlight," the critically acclaimed coming-of-age film set there.

Both filmmakers grew up in the area and are familiar with the poverty and struggle of the public housing projects, but also with their moments of community and beauty.

Above left, Barry Jenkins, who directed, and Tarell Alvin McCraney, who wrote the semi-autobiographical screenplay.

_____

10. Finally, Ronaldo and Messi have some competition coming. From China.

The country's No. 1 fan, President Xi Jinping, is bent on transforming his nation into a great soccer power, spending vast sums to lure talent from Europe and South America and try to upgrade its poor-to-middling rankings.

The real Ronaldo declined an offer of $105 million a year, his agent says.
