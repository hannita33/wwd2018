Here's what you need to know:

Confirmation hearings to begin.

The Senate will hold meetings this week to consider President-elect Donald J. Trump's cabinet nominees. Democrats are seeking to delay the proceedings because the nominees have not all completed background checks and ethics clearances.

A hearing for Senator Jeff Sessions of Alabama, Mr. Trump's choice for attorney general, is scheduled for Tuesday. If Mr. Sessions is confirmed, he is expected to reverse many Obama administration policies on immigration and criminal justice. 

U.S. intelligence findings mocked in Russia.

Russian politicians and commentators ridiculed an American report outlining an elaborate plot ordered by the Kremlin to alter the outcome of the 2016 presidential race, citing a lack of evidence.

Supporters of Mr. Trump in Louisiana and Indiana shared similar views. "What's the big deal?" one asked.

Turkey and Russia deepen relations.

Recent Russian airstrikes to support a Turkish offensive against the Islamic State in Syria signal a remarkable change from November 2015, when a Turkish fighter jet shot down a Russian military plane.

The relationship between the two nations threatens to marginalize the U.S. in efforts to shape Syria's future.

Florida suspect's troubled past.

Esteban Santiago, an Iraq war veteran, is scheduled to appear in court today to face charges related to the fatal shooting of five people at Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport. His relatives say he often spoke about being tormented by voices in his head.

After the shooting, a rumor that there was a second gunman led to more chaos. "Nobody gave us information, and as time went on, we were treated more and more like suspects," one traveler said.

Golden Globe Awards.

"La La Land" won a record seven awards, including for best picture comedy or musical. "Moonlight" was named best picture drama. Here's a complete list of the winners.

The ceremony took a political turn when Meryl Streep used her acceptance speech to denounce the president-elect. Mr. Trump responded to the criticism hours later.

The consequences for the economy are huge, our writer says, and they would be felt "in global financial markets and the aisles of your local Walmart."

The ride-hailing service has created a website that will allow city planners to study traffic patterns that Uber vehicles have collected.

Ties that bind.

Attachment theory states that the quality of our early attachments profoundly influences how we behave as adults. The idea is influencing programs in inner-city preschools as well as executive coaching.

Experts say the theory resonates in an era when people seem more attached to smartphones than to others.

Back Story

If one of your resolutions this year is to eat healthier in the morning, consider porridge.

The combination of grain and water is arguably comfort served in its most humble form. The dish has also been feeding the world for thousands of years.

Congee, a rice-based porridge, has been eaten in China since about 2500 B.C. Koreans make several variations called juk, including one with pine nuts as the main ingredient.

Upma is a thick porridge common in south India made from roasted semolina or rice flour. And in Ethiopia, genfo, unique for its use of red pepper, is a traditional Sunday breakfast.

On many cold mornings, Americans start their days with oatmeal. The actor Wilford Brimley famously pitched the food's health benefits in an ad, saying, "It's the right thing to do."

And oats have been a staple of what is now Scotland since the Roman Empire. A village in the Scottish Highlands even hosts a global porridge-making competition, where the Golden Spurtle is the top prize.

The former oldest living woman in Scotland swore by the dish. Before her death in 2015 at age 109, Jessie Gallan revealed her secret to longevity.

"A nice warm bowl of porridge every morning," she said, and "staying away from men."
