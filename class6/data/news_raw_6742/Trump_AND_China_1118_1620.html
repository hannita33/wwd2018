Facts & Figures: Donald Trump's Long Obsession With People Laughing at America


BYLINE: THE EDITORS


SECTION: OPINION


LENGTH: 152 words



HIGHLIGHT: He&#8217;s brought up the issue at least 107 times over nearly thirty years, according to a Washington Post analysis.


Just how much does Donald Trump talk about people abroad laughing at America? The Washington Post went back through his public statements and found he's brought up the laughing issue at least 103 times, starting as early as 1987. 

Via The Washington Post:

Trump's frequent caution that the world is laughing at us reveals something about his worldview, if not his psyche: A once-exceptional America has transformed into the butt of a global joke, Trump believes, and its reputation must be restored.

It's hard to say how long Trump has sounded the alarm on America's ridicule. (Go ahead, try searching for some combination of "Donald Trump" and "laughing.") But it's fair to say it has been a key concern for the billionaire businessman for at least three decades.

The Post also looked at the countries and groups Mr. Trump has accused of laughing at the United States - top laughers include China, the world, and OPEC.
