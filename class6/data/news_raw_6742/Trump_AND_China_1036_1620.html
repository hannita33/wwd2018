President's Visit Offends Fallen C.I.A. Man's Daughter


SECTION: Section A; Column 0; Editorial Desk; LETTER; Pg. 24


LENGTH: 239 words


To the Editor:

Re ''Slamming Media, Trump Advances Two Falsehoods'' (front page, Jan. 22):  

It was with indignation and disgust that I read that Donald Trump had stood before the Memorial Wall at the C.I.A. headquarters in Langley, Va., and used the occasion to denigrate the free press, one of the pillars of our democracy.

The first star carved on that marble wall commemorates my father, Douglas S. Mackiernan, who gave his life in service to our country in Tibet in 1950. His mission involved monitoring the Soviet Union's initial nuclear explosion from a remote listening post in China.

It is ironic that the new president seems to feel that Russia and its ex-K.G.B. president should be shown great respect, and perhaps even emulated. I take some solace that my father does not have to witness this situation.

Despite Mr. Trump's assertion to the contrary, our country is already great. That greatness has come at significant cost and has required constant sacrifice. As a Gold Star father, Khizr Khan, pointed out, sacrifice is something Mr. Trump knows nothing about.

In the name of my father and the 116 other C.I.A. employees enshrined on the Memorial Wall, in the name of Mr. Khan's son and countless others who made the ultimate sacrifice, we must remain vigilant to see that the greatness of our country is not diminished by this man who unexpectedly found himself in the White House.

MARY MACKIERNAN

Kalaheo, Hawaii
