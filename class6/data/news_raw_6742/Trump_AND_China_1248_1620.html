HIGHLIGHT: Corrections appearing in print on Saturday, December 3, 2016.


FRONT PAGE

An article on Friday about President-elect Donald J. Trump's campaign-style rally in Cincinnati misstated the size of the audience that greeted him. It numbered in the thousands, not the tens of thousands.

A picture caption on Thursday with the continuation of an article about manufacturing jobs in the Midwest misstated in some editions the relationship between the women pictured. Arnita Gray and Carol Bigbee are co-workers at Carrier in Indiana; Ms. Gray is not Ms. Bigbee's daughter. 

An article on Nov. 5 about the chaos of war in the government-held side of Aleppo, Syria - held out by President Bashar al-Assad as safer than the rebel-held side - misstated the surname of name of a man who died at Al Razi hospital in Aleppo while his mother wailed. He was Hazem Sharifa, not Sherif.

INTERNATIONAL

An article on Nov. 4 about the death of two American soldiers and more than 30 Afghans in heavy fighting in Kunduz misstated, in some editions, the month that Taliban insurgents nearly overran Kunduz for a second time. It was October, not November.

An article on Nov. 3 about fears in Hong Kong that Beijing would issue a rare interpretation of Hong Kong's mini-constitution, the Basic Law, to effectively bar two newly elected separatist lawmakers from taking office referred incorrectly, in some editions, to four past interpretations of the Basic Law by China's legislature. Only one of those interpretations came at the request of Hong Kong's highest court; it is not the case that they "customarily" followed such a request. (Four days after the article was published, the Communist Party moved to bar the lawmakers.)

METROPOLITAN

An article last Sunday about New York's disappearing diner culture misidentified the music club at 97th Street and Columbus Avenue that was replaced in the 1990s by the Central Park Cafe/Restaurant. It was Mikell's, not Michael's Pub. Michael's Pub, where Woody Allen used to play clarinet, was on East 55th Street.

BUSINESS DAY

An article on Thursday about China's rethinking of its methods to fight counterfeit goods misstated the given name of a hunter of fake goods. He is Yu Fengxing, not Yu Fengsheng.

SPORTS

The On Baseball column on Friday about Major League Baseball's new collective bargaining agreement described incorrectly, in some copies, how the Yankees would receive a break on their revenue-sharing bill under the deal. A multiplier on revenue based on market size and prior revenue rankings was eliminated; the Yankees did not receive a deduction of their stadium financing costs.

Because of an editing error, a picture caption with an article on Thursday about Chris McCullough, who has bounced between the Brooklyn Nets and the Long Island Nets this season, misidentified him in some copies. He is in the center of the photo taken during a game between the Brooklyn Nets and the Los Angeles Clippers - not on the far left.

An Associated Press report on Monday about the Rangers' 2-0 loss to the Ottawa Senators misstated, in some copies, the number of saves Ottawa's Craig Anderson had. It was 33, not 32.
