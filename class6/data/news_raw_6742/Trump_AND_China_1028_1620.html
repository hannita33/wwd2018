Trump, Sessions, Tillerson: Your Wednesday Evening Briefing


BYLINE: KAREN ZRAICK and LISA IABONI


SECTION: BRIEFING


LENGTH: 763 words



HIGHLIGHT: Here's what you need to know at the end of the day.


(Want to get this briefing by email? Here's the sign-up.)

Good evening. Here's the latest.

1. "I think it was Russia."

That was President-elect Donald Trump at his first news conference since July, conceding for the first time that Russia was behind the hacking of Democrats during the campaign.

Other highlights: he will turn over his business to his two oldest sons and an associate; he insisted Mexico will pay for a border wall; he criticized the pharmaceutical industry, and will seek to "repeal and replace" the Affordable Care Act immediately. 

Here are 10 key moments, fact checks and the transcript.

_____

2. Mr. Trump also lashed out at the media - particularly BuzzFeed, a "failing pile of garbage" - for publishing details of unverified accusations that Russia had compromising information about him.

He praised organizations that declined to publish those details, and vigorously denied the allegations, which were dug up by a former British spy and presented to Mr. Trump by intelligence agencies last week.

The Kremlin's spokesman called it "pulp fiction." Above at center, Buzzfeed's editor in chief, Ben Smith.

_____

3. On Capitol Hill, Rex Tillerson, above, the longtime chief of ExxonMobil nominated for secretary of state, faced sharp questioning from Senator Marco Rubio over his unwillingness to criticize President Vladimir Putin of Russia.

And at another confirmation hearing, Senator Cory Booker broke with tradition by testifying against Senator Jeff Sessions, the nominee for attorney general, rebuking him for opposing immigration, criminal justice reform and laws protecting gays.

_____

4. China sent its sole aircraft carrier into the Taiwan Strait, demonstrating its growing naval strength and signaling an early foreign policy challenge for Mr. Trump.

In response, Taiwan scrambled its fighter jets and deployed a warship. It was the third time in as many days that Chinese military action spurred its neighbors to mobilize fighters.

The show of force, an analyst said, was "worrisome from the U.S. and Taiwan's point of view because we don't know how much more they are going to ratchet up these pressures and tensions."

_____

5. Prosecutors filed criminal charges against six Volkswagen employees for their role in the company's emissions scandal.

One was arrested in Florida last week, but the others are in Germany, which does not normally extradite its citizens.

The company also agreed to a $4.3 billion settlement, bringing the total cost of the deception in the U.S., including settlements of suits by car owners, to $20 billion, making it one of the costliest corporate scandals in history.

Above, Attorney General Loretta Lynch.

_____

6. Dozens of U.S. ambassadors are about to leave office, but few are as prominent as Caroline Kennedy, the daughter of President John F. Kennedy.

During her three years as ambassador to Japan, she went from untested celebrity to respected negotiator.

And as the first woman to hold the post, she encouraged change in a traditionally male-dominated culture.

_____

7. August Wilson, the renowned late playwright and chronicler of the 20th century African-American experience, is having a moment.

The film version of his play "Fences" is in theaters now, starring Denzel Washington and Viola Davis, and his early work "Jitney," above, is about to open on Broadway.

Two of our critics sat down to discuss his work. He "found the divine in the down home," one said.

_____

8. Is Netflix deepening cultural echo chambers

There was a lot to criticize about broadcast TV, but it brought the nation together. Streaming services are doing the opposite, our tech critic writes.

_____

9. Weekend warriors, rejoice.

A new study found that a vigorous once-a-week workout is definitely better than no workout - and maybe even as good as shorter, more frequent ones.

_____

10. Finally, faced with the depths of winter, our Cooking team is going for soup.

They created a comprehensive how-to. It's not just a recipe collection - more like a master class on the fundamental principles of soup creation, and how to apply them to a variety of ingredients.

Happy slurping.
