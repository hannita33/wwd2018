Donald Trump Is Focus of Attacks in the Republican Debate


BYLINE: NICHOLAS KRISTOF


SECTION: OPINION


LENGTH: 1159 words



HIGHLIGHT: The other candidates in Texas were overshadowed as Trump, Marco Rubio and Ted Cruz sparred over immigration, health care and tax returns.


This Republican debate is a make-or-break moment for all candidates but Donald Trump. Among the political experts I speak to, the debate is between those who are betting on Trump and those who think that someone else (usually Rubio) still has a strong chance. The betting markets on the eve of the debate showed Trump with a 74 percent chance of becoming the Republican nominee, and Rubio with a 24 percent chance. Of course Ben Carson and John Kasich are also part of the debate, but the focus is on Trump, Cruz and Rubio as the front-runners. Here's my play-by-play from the debate, in the form not of a column but simply an assortment of my tweets during the debate. It's journalism on the fly - and here goes!
