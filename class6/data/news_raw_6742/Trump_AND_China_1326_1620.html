HIGHLIGHT: A reader says there is no "ethical thicket" to navigate through Congress since it has shown little concern for conflicts of interest.


To the Editor:

Re "A Second Empire by Trump's Side" (front page, Jan. 8): 

In discussing Jared Kushner's dealings with China, the article refers to "the ethical thicket he would have to navigate" while playing a role in the Trump administration and pursuing his business interests. There is no such thicket.

Since neither branch of Congress has shown much concern about conflicts of interest, it is hard to see what obstacles or irritants there might be in his path, as Mr. Kushner, like Donald Trump, uses his extraordinary access and power to enrich himself and his family (to whom he now plans to sell some of his assets).

Unless Congress suddenly reverses itself and develops an interest in ethics, not to mention a spine, Mr. Kushner's path will be clear and untroubled.

KEN BANDES

Jersey City
