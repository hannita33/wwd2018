Why's It Bad If Our Enemies Are Nervous About Trump?


BYLINE:


SECTION: All Editions; Pg. 20


LENGTH: 287 words


The Issue: President Obama's recent claim that foreign leaders say they're concerned about a Trump presidency.

President Obama is correct that Donald Trump has foreign leaders nervous ("Bam: Don has world leaders on edge," May 17).  

The leaders of China, Russia, Iran, ISIS, Cuba and all the other traditional bad guys Obama bows down to are worried that Trump will no longer let them get away with their threats against the United States and our allies.

Steve Becker

East Meadow

Trump is the antithesis of President Obama.

Obama's treasured legacy will melt away very quickly if Trump wins, and he knows it. Already, Obama acts as though he himself is campaigning for a third term.

He knows that if Trump is elected, the last eight years will have been for naught.

Given Obama's famous narcissism, it's possible that Obama may do whatever it takes to keep Trump from the presidency.

John McGinley

Farmingdale, N J

Obama's right. Iran's probably worried about what will happen the next time it abuses US sailors. Russia's no doubt nervous about whether it can continue to buzz US warships and planes. And China may have to stop building military bases in the South China Sea.

I bet Mexico is surely in a tizzy, wondering how on Earth it's going to pay for that border wall.

The fact is, Obama and many foreign leaders share the same fear: Trump will make America great again.

Pete McArdle

Yorktown Heights

Why not have "none of the above" as an option for the upcoming election?

They do it on some standardized tests, so why not try it for this election?

Voters are going to complain, no matter the outcome.

It's worth a try.

Michael Uhila

East Palo Alto, Calif.
